# My Cat V0.2 Playtest Guide

## Who to invite

- Windows users who keep a computer open during work, study, or casual browsing.
- People who live with a cat now or have a clear memory of one cat's daily rhythm.
- A first pass of 5 to 10 people is enough for this prototype.

## Setup

1. Start the app and leave it visible on the desktop.
2. Tell the tester this is a prototype desktop companion, not a pet simulator.
3. Do not explain the menus before the first task.

## Tasks

1. Leave the cat visible while starting another desktop task.
2. Drag it to a place where you want it to stay.
3. Click and pet it once.
4. Pass the mouse near it and see whether it notices you.
5. Tell it that the real cat is sleeping from the cat itself.
6. Tell it that the real cat is playing from the tray.
7. Record the same kind of observation three times in one time period and watch for a learning hint.
8. Turn on quiet mode, then turn it off again.
9. Leave it running while doing another desktop task for at least 30 minutes.

## What to watch

- Whether the transparent desktop presence feels pleasant or distracting.
- Whether the cat menu and tray menu are discovered without coaching.
- Whether dragging it feels like placing a small desktop companion instead of moving a widget.
- Whether sitting, sleeping, walking, window lingering, and petting read as one cat.
- Whether mouse notice and the immediate "tell it" response feel warm without becoming noisy.
- Whether the learned rhythm difference is noticeable without feeling mechanical.
- Whether quiet mode feels calmer rather than simply broken.

## Success signals

- Tester can click, drag, record from both entrances, and exit from the tray.
- Tester describes at least one moment where the cat felt alive on the desktop.
- Tester notices quiet mode making new active behaviors calmer.
- Tester leaves it running instead of closing it immediately.

## Failure signals

- The window blocks normal desktop work.
- The tester cannot find how to teach it.
- The cat still feels fixed in one spot or like a utility widget.
